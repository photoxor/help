Introduction

This document is for Photoxor C1 version 1.4.7 (49), which can be installed or updated for free from Google Play.

Photoxor C1 is a photography tool for exposure, depth of field, flash, filter, CoC calculations, GPS location tracking, ephemeris sun and moon calculations with compass. Visualize locations on a map, and quickly see what's closest to you.

Use the device camera based lightmeter to estimate an Exposure Value (EV) reading, determine camera settings and calculate adjustments needed for a (neutral density) filter stack.

The Depth of Field calculation shows the near and far limits of sharpness, and additionally provides a graphical visualization of the level of sharpness and diffraction. The Circle of Confusion (CoC) is automatically calculated from the camera sensor size, but may be overridden with a custom value. A special CoC calculation screen allows the selection of camera and print specification. It determines the required sensor CoC and print resolution (DPI) depending on the anticipated minimum viewing distance.

The Depth of Field calculation also allows specifying the range of sharpness (near and far limits) and calculates the needed camera settings.

Location tracking is available to record your path of travel. You can also record audio notes to quickly store information on your way. For reference you may want to take geotagged photographs with your device camera.

Ephemeris sun and moon calculations allow you to determine Golden and Blue Hours, direction of sun and moon and much more.

An application widget allows quick access to Logging and Tracking, and also provides a Torch function.

A number of in-app purchases are available to further extend capabilities.

For latest information, tips and tricks please visit the Photoxor Blog.

The startup screen presents a number of icons as shortcuts for access to functionality of the application. The default arrangement might not be the best for your particular usage, and therefore prefer to rearrange icons. To do this, press and hold on the icon you want to move. Photography Calculations

A set of screen is available to perform photography calculations, including exposure, filter adjustments, depth of field, field of view, circle of confusion.

Exposure (EV)

This screen enables you to perform exposure calculations in two ways: calculate the exposure value from your camera settings, or - in reverse - enter an exposure value (use the lightmeter for an indicative value) and determine the best camera settings.

EV from Camera Settings

Enter your camera settings

  • aperture (f/x),
  • exposure time in seconds,
  • film speed (ISO).

The corresponding calculated exposure value (EV) is now shown below, together with a short description and an image to indicate a typical scenario. The luminance value is expressed in candela per square meter (cd/m2).

To refine the values use the Locks mechanism.

Go to Settings for a change of the Stop system (value increments, for example values in full stops only or 1/3 stops).

Exposure Value (EV)

In preparation of a photo shoot you might already know the exposure value of the scenario. In such case use the slider to enter the exposure value. The camera settings will be adjusted accordingly.

Press the lock symbol to prevent the EV from changing its value (please note that for some extreme settings the EV changes due to range limits).

To refine the values use the Locks mechanism.

Locks

Locks are used to prevent a value from changing. A lock can be open or closed.

Please note that on this screen only two of the four locks can be in a locked state. When you change one of the unlocked values, the other unlocked value will be adjusted accordingly.

Lightmeter

There are two types of lightmeter function available. One uses the device camera, the other the device light sensor.The buttons are only enabled if the device is fitted with camera / sensor.

If your device is equipped with a camera, you may use it as a simple light meter. The light meter takes a picture and sets the EV according to the exposure details of the image. The EV will become locked. The accuracy of the recorded exposure value (EV) depends on your device.

If your device is equipped with a light sensor, you may use this sensor to measure illuminance. Press the Light Sensor button and a dialog window is shown. The measured light intensity is shown in lux together with the calculated exposure value (EV). Once you accept the measurement, the EV is set and become locked. Camera settings are adjusted to reflect the EV.

Filter Stack (FILTER)

The Filter screen is used to adjust camera settings by the amount of light reduction from one or more attached neutral density (ND) filters. This is in particular useful for very dark filters, often called 'Black Glass', where exposure measurement through the DSLR camera is not possible.

An article with a step by step guide can be found on the Photoxor Blog.

Camera Settings without Filter

Here you enter the camera settings without the filter(s) attached. You can use the Exposure (EV) screen to calculate these settings, and then move to this Filter screen.

TIp: In case a filter is attached already (such as a protective or UV filter), there is not need to remove, just don't add to the filter stack.

User Presets

User exposure settings allow you to quickly preset your preferred camera settings for various lighting conditions. The free version gives you two settings using the blue buttons. Purchasing the Filter Pro add-on enables the use of named presets, which are access with the golden button.

To recall a setting perform a 'short' click, which shows a dialog to select one of the name stored settings. You are also able to add the current setting as a new preset or go to the list of presets.

Filter Stack

Now enter the neutral density filters you intend to mount on you camera. The free version allows to set one filter only, whereas the 'Filter-Stack' in-app purchase supports a stack of multiple filters and also fractional light reduction values.

Then Set Camera To

Here you find the camera setting reflecting the light reduction once the filters are attached. Using these settings gives your image about the same exposure level as using the original settings without filter.

In case you find a difference in exposure, either the light situation might have changed (for example during sunset), or the filter light reduction values are not exactly correct. In case you require more precise values you should perform some tests beforehand and adjust the light reduction value within the filter definition.

Exposure Timer

For exposure times (with filter attached) of 3 or more seconds a countdown timer is available. This timer can be started by pressing the button. Then a dialog window is presented for the countdown. When finished a notification is performed. This can be configured in Settings as sounding a notification ringtone, vibrate or switching on the flash LED (if available). The countdown may be cancelled using the Cancel button at any time.

This timer is in particular useful in case your camera does not provide a built-in setting for the calculated exposure time. Then set the camera to BULB mode and start the timer when you press the shutter release. When the timer raises the notification alarm press the camera release to close the shutter.

Depth of Field (DOF)

This screen allows to calculate the Depth of Field (DoF) for a given scenario.

For this calculation you select your camera, aperture, focal length, distance and optionally adjust the Circle of Confusion (CoC) value (for advanced scenarios use the CoC calculation screen). The depth of field is then shown with near and far distance values. The hyperfocal length is calculated, too.

For focal length and also distance you can either use the (logarithmic) sliders to quickly set approximate values or use the entry fields for a precise value. Distance values can be shown and entered either in meter or feet.

The Circle of Confusion (CoC) is determined from the camera details based on the Zeiss formula, but you may select your own value.

Please note that near, far and hyperfocal distances are currently calculated based on the set CoC value without consideration of diffraction.

The graph presents three CoC lines depending on the distance:

  • the calculated CoC value for the distance (yellow line),
  • the CoC plus diffraction (dotted red line),
  • the reference CoC (black dotted line) as the (sometimes a bit arbitrary) boundary between 'sharp' and 'blur'.

The distance value where the CoC value is below the reference value is considered as 'sharp'; lower CoC values indicate higher sharpness.

Use this graph to quickly visualize changes to the sharpness range.

Press on the graph area to show or hide the legend.

In case you need to define a new camera characteristics or amend, press the settings button next to the camera. Also go to Settings for a change of the aperture value increment (for example full stops only or 1/3 stops).

Depth of Field - Near / Far (DOF NEAR/FAR)

This Depth of Field calculation is often also called 'reverse DoF calculation'. It allows you to specify a distance range where you require sharpness, and calculates camera settings.

Please note that such calculation works well for 'realistic' values, but might fail for more extreme scenarios.

The graph is the same as for the standard DoF calculation.

CoC

The CoC value used for Depth of Field calculations is traditionally based on the Zeiss formula, which uses a print size of 30cm x 20cm viewed from 25cm distance (the comfortable viewing distance for the human eye). For general calculations this CoC value is often satisfactory.

To produce high-quality prints a more refined CoC value is required. This calculation screen removes guessing, and allows to perform non-standard CoC calculations based on a camera sensor and print specification. It determines the required sensor CoC and print resolution (DPI) depending on the anticipated minimum viewing distance. And it helps you to also understand a scenario where the print resolution is a given factor.

To calculate a CoC value specific to your requirements first select your camera. Important is the correct sensor size. Use the icon next to the camera selection if you would like to create a new camera setting or amend an existing one.

Next you need to decide on the print size of the planned image. Here as well, use the icon next to the print selection if you would like to create a new print definition or amend an existing one. Most important is the size of the print.

Usually the calculation is based on the width, but you may alternatively use height or diagonal. Depending on you preference, choose your unit of measurement.

The application now calculates the 'comfortable' viewing distance for a human eye. This is based on the viewing angle. If you have purchased the high-precision extension, you are able to amend the angle (standard is 60 degree) in Settings.

It also calculates the minimum required print resolution in Dots per Inch (DPI), and the required sensor CoC value. This calculation is also based on the human eye resolution. The standard value is 5 lp/mm. If you have purchased the high-precision extension, you are able to amend this value in Settings.

The next section allows you to define a minimum viewing distance. The viewer of your print might first observe at the 'comfortable' viewing distance, but then get closer to inspect details. If you use the CoC standard calculation the sharpness then might not be satisfactory any more. Enter the anticipated minimum viewing distance and get the required print resolution (DPI) and CoC.

The following section is based on the print resolution. For a given print resolution you are able to determine what would be the minimum acceptable viewing distance and the matching CoC.

Field of View (FOV)

How far do you need to stand back to capture a large building? Here the Field of View calculation screen is used.

Select the type of camera used and the focal length of the lens; this determines the angle of view, which is shown for both width and height. Now increase or decrease the distance until you get a Height value larger than the building height.

In reverse, if you have got a zoom lens, enter your distance from the object and change the focal length until the Height value is larger than the building height.

The Field of View screen supports both dimensional and angular calculation.

As for most of the other calculation screens, the focus distance can be either set using the (logarithmic) slider or the entry field. The focal length can be set in the same way.

Distance values can be entered and shown either in meter or feet.

In case you need to define a new camera characteristics, press the settings button next to the camera and add a new one.

Flash

The Flash screen helps to perform flash related calculations to determine the reach of a flash unit.

First select the flash unit and guide number profile, and you get the guide number for this configuration. If needed you are able to manually change the guide number value. Next choose the output power, film speed (ISO) and aperture, and receive the distance.

You may display distance related values (i.e. distance and guide number) in either Meter or Feet.

Please note that without the Flash Pro add-on only one guide number profile can be set (exception are the sample flash definitions).

In case you need to define a new flash characteristics, press the settings button and add a new flash.

Tracking and Ephemeris

Use GPS tracking to log your movement path to a file or display on a map. Find sun and moon directions with the Ephemeris screen.

Four screens (tabs) are available: TRACKING, MAP, PLACES, EPHEMERIS.

TRACKING Screen

From the Startup screen press the Tracking icon to reach the tracking functionality.

The tracking screen offers two key functionalities:

Tracking
A location provider (such as GPS) is used to determine the current location coordinates, and often also other details such as altitude, speed and bearing of movement. This information is shown on the screen and updated when available.
For GPS coordinates - depending on your device - it may take some time until the first fix is locked. In some instances, in particular within buildings you might not receive a GPS fix at all.
The location provider can be changed in Settings, also while Logging is active.
Logging
The current location information is recorded. It can be stored to a file where both GPX and KML formats are supported, or shown on the MAP screen only.
When you switch on Logging, the Tracking functionality is automatically enabled as well. You are able to switch off Tracking while Logging continues to be active. This can be used to save energy for example while inside a building or stationary at a location for some time.

While Logging is active both audio recordings and photos (taken with device camera) can be associated with the current location. You might use this feature to quickly record information about a place. For such recordings a marker is shown on the map.

Audio Recording

Wile Logging is active you are able to record an audio message. This recording is geocoded with the current location. In case the track is presented on the map (MAP screen) then a marker icon in shape of a microphone is shown.

Within Settings you can change the recording format between 3GP, MPEG4, AMR. The recording file is stored in a folder for media files associated with the track. The name is shown during the audio recording dialog.

Finish Logging

When you finish logging a dialog appears to select what action to take. Use Cancel in case you switched off logging in error.

You can share the log file, for example upload to cloud storage, send via email, ...

Depending on your settings (see Settings) the log file together with audio recordings and photos will be packaged as a ZIP file.

Please note that in case no records (for example no GPS fix could be obtained yet) are logged when you stop, no data from that session are available for sharing. Therefore in such situation pressing Share brings up a file dialog. You can then either select an old recording for sharing or abort.

The following log file formats are supported:

GPX
The GPX file format records the path with timestamp, location coordinates, speed, course, elevation.
This format is suitable for input into tools such as Adobe Lightroom.
KML
The KML file format records the path with timestamp and location coordinates.
This format is suitable for Google applications.

MAP Screen

The MAP screen is based on Google Maps. It allows you to display the recorded path, a previously logged path, or mark places (see PLACES screen). It also allows you to present sun and moon data, such as the directions for rise and set of sun and moon.

To display the map, Internet connectivity is required. You may use the Google Map application to persist a map area for offline access.

EPHEMERIS Screen

The ephemeris screen provides you with a wide variety of sun and moon related calculations. It includes rise and set times and directions, twilight times, moon phase.

For these calculations you need to set both the geographic location and time/date.

The compass rose visualises the rise, set and time specific directions, and also indicates the altitude above / below horizon for sun and moon throughout a day.

The compass rose turns to show the actual direction. Please note that this compass functionality is only available if your device has the necessary sensors builtin. The accuracy of directions depends on surrounding influences such as magnetic and electrical devices.

Ephemeris related settings are available to customise the colors used.

First you need to choose a location. There are multiple options to do this:

Drop-Down Selection:
Use the drop-down to select a predefined location. There are also two special location types: Current GPS Location and User Location.
In case the location of interest isn't already defined, you may define either a User Location or press the icon to open the locations list to enter the location definition. Or start a location search by pressing the icon.
Current GPS Location:
In case a GPS location is available it can be selected for the Ephemeris calculations. Please note that the Ephemeris screen is a passive listener to GPS locations (which does not draw extra power), and therefore only receives location information if requested by some other activity (other application or tracking is enable on TRACKING screen).
Once a GPS location is set, the position latitude and longitude coordinates are shown.
User Location
A User Location is a temporary location, which can be set through a location search or by moving the location marker on the MAP screen.

Once a location is defined, the timezone will be set where possible. Alternatively you might select from the timezone drop-down (which is a quite long list).

Important: Times shown on this screen are expressed in terms of this timezone, except for the graph where the location location timezone is applied!

Next set the date and time for this calculation using the buttons. To get the current date / time press .

Graph

The graph presents the sun and moon altitudes throughout a day. The current time is marked by a vertical line. A slider below the graph allows to change the time of the day for the calculation.

Compass Rose

The compass rose presents:

  • Compass direction (if enabled using the toggle ON / OFF button just above the compass rose)
  • Sun rise direction
  • Sun set direction
  • Sun direction at specified time of day (with sun symbol)
  • Sun altitude throughout the day
  • Moon rise direction
  • Moon set direction
  • Moon direction at specified time of day (with moon symbol)
  • Moon altitude throughout the day

Please note that an altitude below horizon is shown as dashed line.

Colors can be changed using the icon below the compass rose.

To save batterie power you are able to switch off the compass direction movement of the rose. When switched off the northern direction is always show on top. In Settings you are able to configure the application to automatically switch off the compass if the battery level is low.

While the compass is on, the strength of the detected magnetic field is shown in micro tesla. Typical earth magnetic field readings are in a range between 30 and 60 micro tesla. The value is shown in red when outside this range. In particular higher values indicate other magnetic or electric sources. When using the compass, to get a good direction accuracy please ensure that you are away from magnetic or electric sources.

Settings - Cameras
Camera definitions are used for various calculations, such as field of view, but also to determine the circle of confusion (CoC) for Depth of Field (DoF) calculations.
Sensor
The sensor size is defined in millimeter (mm).
Lens
In case your camera is fitted with a fixed lens, you are able to define the basic characteristics, which is then used to populate screens accordingly.

For a fixed lens, tick the checkbox and enter the minimum and maximum focal length. In case of a prime lens, enter the same value in both fields. The focal length is

entered in millimeter (mm).

CoC
The default CoC value is calculated from the sensor size based on the Zeiss formula and shown in micrometer (μm).
You may define your own CoC value, entered in micrometer (μm).
Settings - Filters
Filter definitions are used in exposure calculations to determine the level of light reductions. Currently all filters are treated as neutral density (ND) filters characterized by the level of light reduction.
F-Stop Reduction
The reduction of light is entered in the number stops.
If you purchased to Filter Stack option, then a fractional stop value can be entered and used for the calculation.

Settings - Flashes

Flash definitions are used for calculation of their reach of illumination.

Profile Name
The name of a guide number profile. Use this name to describe the specific flash setting, such as Diffuser Dome attached, or for zoom flash units the focal length.
GN
The guide number (for metric system).
F/L
Optional, the associated focal length in millimeters. This value is set for zoom type flash units only.

Settings - Exposure Presets

Exposure presets are used to quickly access preferred camera settings for specific lighting situations.

Aperture
The aperture value.
Exposure Time
The exposure time.
Film Speed
The film speed (ISO).
EV
The EV value calculated from the set aperture, exposure time, film speed.
Settings - Print Definitions

Print definitions are used for the specialized calculation of the circle of confusion (CoC) for Depth of Field (DoF) based on both print and camera sensor characteristics.

Size
The print size is defined by width and height in meter.
Resolution
The print resolution is entered in dots per inch (DPI).
The resolution is used to calculate a minimum viewing distance, where the human eye considers the print still as sharp. It is also used to calculate the corresponding sensor CoC.
Viewing Distance
The anticipated closest viewing distance is entered in meter.
By default the CoC calculation uses the natural human viewing distance based on the 'comfortable' viewing angle of the human eye. This is often a good value, but in some circumstance you may want to provide an explicit minimum viewing distance. For example, the viewer is unable to get any closer to the print, and therefor the sharpness requirements are less. Alternatively, you might anticipate the view to perform a 'close inspection' and still would like to present the image with full sharpness.
Notes
You may enter some notes to describe the print definition.
This information is not used for calculation purposes.
Settings - Location Definitions

Locations are used for ephemeris calculations and can be defined using latitude and longitude coordinates.

Location Name
A name for the location, such as the address.
In case you don't want to manually enter location coordinates, use the search feature.
Optionally enter the search criteria in the location name field and press the search icon . This will open the search text field and present you with search resultts. On selection of a result from the list the location will be shown on the map screen.
Timezone
The timezone of the location.
After the coordinates (longitude and latitude) are entered press the Set button to automatically retrieve the timezone for the location. Please note that daylight saving is date specific and the current date will be used.
Longitude
The longitude of the location.
The entry format is dd:mm:ss.ss
where dd are degrees, mm are minutes, ss.ss are seconds, which could be a fractional value.
Latitude
The latitude of the location.
The entry format is the same as for longitude.
Elevation
The optional elevation of the location in meter above sea level.
Please note that this field is for information purposes only and currently not used for calculations.
Notes
An optional description.
This field is for information purposes only and not used for calculations.

Settings - Comfortable viewing angle

The human 'comfortable' viewing angle for the free version is 60 degrees. The High-Precision purchasable feature allows to configure the angle.

Settings - Human eye resolution

The human eye resolution for the free version is 5 lp/mm. The High-Precision purchasable feature allows to configure the resolution.

Settings - Configuration

Precision

Calculation precision for the free version is 3 significant digits. The High-Precision purchasable feature allows to configure the precision between 2 and 8 digits.

Stop scale

This setting allows the configuration of the stop scale, such as using full, 1/4, 1/3, 1/2 stops.

Unit of Measurement

This setting allows to change between Meter and Feet as the unit of measurement for distance.

Film / sensor speed ('ISO') type

This setting allows configuration of the available ISO values. It can be restricted to the ISO 12232:2006 standard values, or extended to include camera manufacturer values.

Wavelength

This setting allows configuration of the light wavelength used for the Airy Disk calculation used to show the diffraction influence in Depth of Field calculations. The value is in nanometer.

Screen

This setting provides you with configuration of the application screens.

You are able to customize the screen background and select either the default background or change to a custom color using the color picker. It is recommended to use a light color to retain good visibility of the application screen information.

Settings - Exposure Timer

Notification Sound

Settings - Rewards

Reward points can be earned by completing surveys and viewing other advertisement content.

After earning some points you can use them to activate otherwise chargeable functionality for a limited time.

The Earn Points screen lists surveys and advertisement. Click on one of the active buttons and complete the flow to earn the shown number of points. As soon as you've earned points the total points held in your wallet will be updated.

Please note that some of the content may not be available, and the button remains inactive. As soon as content becomes available the associated button will become active.

Once you have earned some points, redeem them by subscribing to one or more additional application functionality. On selection of one of the application features available to the rewards system, a Reward Detail screen is shown. This screen provides you with a feature description and your current subscription status for this feature, as well as the number of points required for a 24 hours activation.

The Cancel button can be used to move back to the list screen without making any changes.

Use the Subscribe button to subscribe to the application feature. Once subscribed, the feature will be activated as soon as sufficient points are in your Wallet. As long as you maintain sufficient points in your Wallet the application feature will be reactivated on expiry of the 24 hours activation, and the number of points will be deducted accordingly.

You may unsubscribe from the application feature at any time using the Stop button. The subscription will be ended, and access to the feature remains active until expiry of the activation period. You may subscribe to the feature again when needed.
